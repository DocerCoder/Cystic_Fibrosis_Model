🧠 PART 1: AI-Driven Research Workflow for Stratacyst
🔧 Step 1: Define Your Core Research Questions
Before you query any AI tool, clarify what you want to learn. These are your anchor questions:
What are the latest findings on CFTR mutation classes and their correlation with phenotype?
How do sweat chloride, BMI, lung function, infections, and comorbidities predict CF severity?
What are the limitations of genotype-only diagnostics, and how can phenotype-first modeling improve accuracy?
What machine learning models have been used to infer genotype or predict CF risk?
What are the modulator eligibility criteria, and how can phenotype data guide treatment decisions?
You’ll use these questions across multiple tools.
🔧 Step 2: Use Consensus for Evidence-Based Answers
Tool: Consensus
Go to the site and enter one question at a time (e.g., “Do sweat chloride levels correlate with CFTR mutation class?”)
Review the summary, then click into the source papers
Copy key findings and note the citation info (author, journal, year)
💡 Use Consensus to validate your app’s logic and get peer-reviewed support for your modeling assumptions.
🔧 Step 3: Use Elicit for Structured Paper Extraction
Tool: Elicit
Enter: “Phenotype-first modeling in Cystic Fibrosis”
Use filters: 2022–2025, clinical trials, machine learning, CFTR
For each paper, extract:
Abstract
Methods
Results
Limitations
Save the structured data—it’s perfect for your Methods and Results sections
💡 Elicit helps you build your report’s backbone without reading full papers.
🔧 Step 4: Use SciSpace for Deep Literature Search
Tool: SciSpace
Search: “CFTR genotype-phenotype correlation”
Use the PDF reader to highlight key graphs, tables, and citations
Save visualizations for your report (e.g., mutation class vs. severity)
💡 SciSpace is ideal for finding figures and tables to include in your ISEF presentation.
🔧 Step 5: Use Research Rabbit to Map Citation Networks
Tool: Research Rabbit
Create a project: “Stratacyst CF Inference”
Add key papers from Consensus or SciSpace
Explore related papers, co-authors, and citation clusters
Identify emerging research trends or gaps Stratacyst can fill
💡 This helps you show judges how your app fits into the broader research ecosystem.
🔧 Step 6: Use Claude or Copilot to Draft Your Report
Once you’ve gathered findings:
Ask: “Write a research report introduction explaining phenotype-first CF modeling and Stratacyst’s innovation”
Then: “Summarize the methods used in recent CF risk modeling papers”
Then: “Compare Stratacyst to CFTR2 and PROMISE tools in a table”
Then: “Generate MLA citations for these 20 sources”
💡 I can help you write and polish every section—just drop the findings here.
🔧 Step 7: Use Wordvice.ai  to Polish Academic Tone
Tool: Wordvice AI
Paste your draft
Select: Academic Writing
Choose: Scientific Report
Review suggestions for clarity, tone, and formatting
💡 This ensures your writing meets ISEF and university-level standards.
🔧 Step 8: Use Zotero + Elephas to Organize Citations
Use Zotero to store all your sources
Use Elephas to annotate and tag findings by category (e.g., “modulator eligibility,” “phenotype modeling”)
💡 This makes it easy to build your Works Cited section and defend your research.

A full research report with citations, graphs, and structured findings

Evidence-backed claims that judges and CF experts will respect
A ready-to-present demo and research package for UW collaboration
