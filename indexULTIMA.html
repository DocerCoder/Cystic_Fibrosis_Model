Cystic Fibrosis overview

Cystic Fibrosis (CF) is a genetic condition caused by pathogenic variants in CFTR, leading to multisystem disease. Clinical presentation varies with CFTR dysfunction level, environment, and care.

CFTR classes

I: No protein; II: Misfolding/processing (e.g., F508del); III: Gating (e.g., G551D); IV: Conductance (e.g., R117H); V: Reduced synthesis.

Phenotype signals

Sweat chloride (≥60 typical CF-range), FEV₁% predicted, chronic infections, BMI/nutrition, CFRD, pancreatic status.

Modulators

Ivacaftor benefits gating; ETI benefits many class II contexts; indications are genotype-specific and evolving.

Why inference matters

When genotype is unknown, phenotype patterns can guide provisional expectations and education. Stratacyst shows how inputs drive outputs for transparent learning.
