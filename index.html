Welcome to Stratacyst

Stratacyst estimates CF risk and severity, infers likely CFTR genotype class from phenotype, and suggests modulator eligibility. It’s transparent, educational, and designed for scenario simulation—no backend, all local.

What it does

Combines sweat chloride, lung function, infections, BMI, and comorbidities to compute a risk score and severity index.

Why it helps

Phenotype-first inference supports users who don’t know genotype, while optional genotype refines eligibility.

How to use

Go to “New patient,” enter phenotype, optionally genotype, then click “Compute estimates.”

Cystic Fibrosis overview

Cystic Fibrosis (CF) is a genetic condition caused by pathogenic variants in CFTR, leading to multisystem disease. Clinical presentation varies with CFTR dysfunction level, environment, and care.

CFTR classes

I: No protein; II: Misfolding/processing (e.g., F508del); III: Gating (e.g., G551D); IV: Conductance (e.g., R117H); V: Reduced synthesis.

Phenotype signals

Sweat chloride (≥60 typical CF-range), FEV₁% predicted, chronic infections (e.g., Pseudomonas), BMI/nutrition, CFRD, pancreatic status.

Modulators

Ivacaftor benefits gating; ETI benefits many class II contexts; indications are genotype-specific and evolving.

Why inference matters

When genotype is unknown, phenotype patterns can guide provisional expectations and education. Stratacyst shows how inputs drive outputs for transparent learning.
